RECIPE 0125 min

Dadi's Soft Phulka

— as made every evening, for as long as anyone remembers You'll need
  • 2 cups Saral Protein Atta+
  • 3/4 cup warm water, added slowly
  • 1/2 tsp ghee, for the dough
  • Extra ghee, for finishing
Method
  • Knead into a soft, slightly sticky dough; rest 20 minutes covered with a damp cloth
  • Roll thin and even — granny always said an uneven phulka puffs unevenly
  • Cook on a hot tava till bubbles form, then finish briefly over open flame
  • Brush with ghee and stack in a cloth-lined dabba to stay soft
Granny's tip: rest the dough, don't rush it — that's what keeps it soft till the last roti, not just the first.
RECIPE 0235 min

Nani's Methi Thepla

— the one that travels well in a tiffin You'll need
  • 2 cups Saral Protein Atta+
  • 1 cup chopped fresh methi leaves
  • 2 tbsp curd, 1 tsp ajwain, 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 2 tbsp oil, salt to taste, water as needed
Method
  • Mix atta with methi, curd and spices; bring together with a little water into a firm dough
  • Rest 15 minutes so the methi softens into the dough
  • Roll medium-thick and cook on a hot tava with a little oil on each side, till golden-spotted
  • Serve warm, or pack once fully cooled
Nani's tip: a spoon of curd in the dough keeps thepla soft even hours later, no extra ghee needed.
RECIPE 0320 min

Amma's Jaggery Roti

— the after-school one, still warm from the tava You'll need
  • 2 cups Saral Protein Atta+
  • 3 tbsp grated jaggery, dissolved in warm milk
  • 1 tbsp ghee
  • A pinch of cardamom powder
Method
  • Warm the milk just enough to melt the jaggery completely, then cool slightly
  • Knead atta with the jaggery-milk, ghee and cardamom into a smooth dough
  • Roll gently — this dough tears more easily than a plain one — and cook on medium heat
  • Finish both sides with a light brush of ghee till lightly caramelised
Amma's tip: medium heat, more patience — jaggery catches fast on a too-hot tava.
